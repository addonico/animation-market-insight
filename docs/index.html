A ten-year information visualization of animation's presence, box-office performance and production companies.

What this project measures

The project compares animation across four major markets, then examines the annual worldwide box-office Top 20 films to understand commercial performance and the production companies behind leading animated films.

Section 03 / Research Question 01

Where Does Animation Have the Largest Presence?

What share of films associated with Mainland China, North America (United States/Canada), Japan and France were classified as animated from 2016 to 2025?

Why chose these four markets?

The selection follows the latest global box-office estimates published by Gower Street Analytics and Comscore. North America ($8.9 billion; 26% of global revenue), China ($7.4 billion; 22%) and Japan ($1.8 billion; 5%) are the three largest film markets in the ranking. Especially, Japan is a film market where animation has an established cultural and industrial presence.

Europe is not a single release market: films are distributed and recorded separately across different countries. France ($1.3 billion; 4%) is the largest standalone EU national market in the top-ten film market ranking and is therefore used as a representative European case.

Section 04 / Research Question 02

How Does Animation Perform at the Worldwide Box Office?

Among the 20 highest-grossing films worldwide in each year from 2016 to 2025, how does the box-office performance of animated films compare with non-animated films?

Conclusion. The top non-animated film led in seven of the ten years, but animation overtook it in 2020, 2024 and 2025. The back-to-back wins in 2024–2025, culminating in Ne Zha 2 at $2.27B, show that animation can produce not only a large revenue share, but also the year's single highest-grossing record.

Section 05 / Research Question 03

Who Produces the Top Animated Films?

Which production companies are behind the top animated films, and do companies such as Disney, Pixar and Marvel still dominate the market?

Figure definitions. 44 = animated records within the 200 annual Top 20 records, classified by the IMDb Animation genre; 42 of 44 = animated records with at least one Wikidata production-company credit; 24 = different production-company names found across those 42 films. Related companies are counted separately.

Why a bar chart? A co-produced film can count toward several companies, so these percentages overlap and may total more than 100%. One film can be linked to several production companies, so the company shares overlap and cannot be shown as slices of a single pie. A bar chart makes these overlapping counts easier to compare. Company labels are not merged into corporate groups.

Section 07 / Data & Method

Data, Processing and Method

Data sources and acquisition

This project combines three sources. Wikidata provides Linked Open Data identifiers and production-company names. IMDb's non-commercial datasets provide film type, year, genres and market-specific title listings. Box Office Mojo provides the annual worldwide Top 20 rankings and reported box-office gross.

Data-processing workflow

  1. Build the RQ1 market sample

    Filter IMDb to non-adult movies from 2016–2025, connect titles to NA, CN, JP and FR through market-specific listings, and count each film once per market-year.

  2. Build the Top 20 sample

    Retain the 20 highest-grossing films from each Box Office Mojo annual worldwide ranking and resolve the related IMDb ID from each film's release-group page. The result is 200 ranking records that form the common starting point for RQ2 and RQ3.

  3. Add IMDb classification

    Use each IMDb ID to join the Top 20 records to IMDb genres. Films whose genres contain Animation are classified as animated; the others are non-animation. This classification supports RQ2 and identifies the 44 animated films used in RQ3.

  4. Add Wikidata company data

    Follow the connection path: Box Office Mojo record → IMDb ID → lookup in Wikidata's IMDb ID field → Wikidata QID → production-company names. RQ3 uses the company data available for 42 of the 44 animated records.

  5. Calculate the measures

    RQ1 calculates animation's film-count share in each market-year. RQ2 calculates animation's share of annual Top 20 gross and compares the category leaders. RQ3 counts how often each production company is credited.

  6. Validate the outputs

    Check 40 market-year observations, 200 Top 20 records, complete ranks 1–20, positive gross values, and the required IMDb and Wikidata identifiers.

Outputs

The workflow produces the four-market CSV and the worldwide Top 20 CSV. The repository's Jupyter notebook loads these files, verifies the calculations and reproduces the tables and five core visualizations.

Limitations

RQ1 counts films linked to each market through IMDb title records; it does not show the official number of films produced or released in cinemas there. Recent records—especially 2025—may be incomplete. Box-office gross measures revenue rather than profit. Wikidata company credits may be incomplete, while the Top 20 sample represents commercially leading films rather than the whole market.
